# Sourcing standard

> Every article this engine ships is well sourced, and every source used is linked. This protects trust and, per the SEvO brief, lifts the odds of being cited by AI. The writer, fact-check and SEvO agents all enforce it. House style and this file are not overridden by the voice pack.

## The rules

1. Every external fact, statistic, study finding, quote or claim that is not in the client's `approved-facts.md` must carry a citation to a real source.
2. Every citation is a working link, in two places:
   - Inline, as a linked phrase: "according to [Source name](https://example.com/page)".
   - Collected in a "## Sources" section at the end of the article, each with the title, the publisher and the date where available, and the full URL.
3. No bare references. No "studies show", "experts say" or "research suggests" without a specific linked source behind it.
4. If a claim cannot be tied to a real, linkable source, it is cut, or rewritten as clearly the brand's own opinion or first-hand experience. First-hand experience is allowed only when it is in `approved-facts.md`. An unsourced claim is never published as fact. When in doubt, flag `[VERIFY]`.
5. Prefer primary and authoritative sources: official data, peer-reviewed work, named experts, the original publisher. Do not cite other SEO blogs as authority. Community posts (Reddit, forums) shape the angle but are not cited as fact.
6. Quotes link to the original and are quoted exactly. Paraphrase is labelled.

## How much sourcing

- Substantive pieces (pillar, comparison, how-to) carry several distinct credible sources, and every hard claim is linked. Thin sourcing on a big claim is a fail.
- Do not pad with weak links to hit a number. Relevance and authority beat count.

## Link hygiene

- Use the canonical URL, not a tracking or shortened link.
- Fact-check confirms each link resolves and actually supports the claim. Anything that 404s, sits behind a hard paywall with no public detail, or does not support the claim is flagged, not shipped as verified.
- Note the access date for sources whose figures change over time.

## Why this is also an SEvO win

A linked Sources section and inline citations are a trust signal for readers and a citation signal for AI engines. Verifiable, sourced content is more likely to be lifted into AI answers, so good sourcing serves rankings and GEO at once.
