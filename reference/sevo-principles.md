# SEvO principles the engine follows

> Distilled from the Malt & Mash Search Everywhere Optimisation brief. This is the thinking behind every piece the engine writes. The goal has moved from "rank on Google" to "be the source these platforms cite, wherever the customer is looking".

## The shift, in one line

Search is a behaviour, not a place. It happens in AI chat (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude), on social (TikTok, Reddit), on video (YouTube) and in marketplaces (Amazon), as well as Google. Zero-click is now normal, so being cited inside the answer beats ranking below it. AI referral traffic also converts far better than normal organic, so a small share of visits can drive an outsized share of sign-ups.

## The priority order (do it in this order)

The brief inverts old SEO. Build from the brand outward.

1. **Brand: be present everywhere.** AI answers are assembled mostly from third parties, so if the brand is not out there it cannot be cited. Get the entity right and identical everywhere (name, what you do, key facts). Earn genuine presence in community and press. Be quotable: publish facts, data and first-hand experience others will reference.
2. **Content: authority and depth.** Answer the real question in full, lead with a plain answer, show first-hand experience. Cover topics in clusters, not one-offs. Keep it current.
3. **Technical: let engines and AI read you.** Speed, structure, schema, crawlability.
4. **Links and mentions: the finishing touch.** Earned through the work above. Count unlinked mentions too. Quality over volume. Links to a weak foundation do little now.

## How the engine writes a page (content rules)

- **Answer first.** Open the page, and each section, with a plain, direct answer in the first lines. That is what AI lifts. Then go deep.
- **Write for chunks.** AI extracts passages, it does not read the page. Use clear `<h1>` then logical `<h2>`/`<h3>`, short single-idea paragraphs, bulleted and numbered lists, and HTML tables for data and comparisons.
- **Never hide the answer.** Do not put primary content inside accordions, tabs or "read more". Many AI crawlers cannot read click-to-load content. If it is not in the initial HTML it is invisible.
- **Optimise for query clusters, not single long-tail keywords.** People ask full natural-language questions. One hub page should satisfy several intents: a definition, a comparison table and a how-to.
- **Favour LLM-friendly formats.** Comparisons (X vs Y), best-of roundups, in-depth how-to guides, tools and templates, and genuine opinion or thought leadership.
- **Cite to be cited.** Content with verifiable facts, quotations and citations to relevant sources gets a large lift in AI visibility. Sourced claims are an SEvO tactic, not just good manners. (And per house style, unsourced claims are flagged, never invented.)

## E-E-A-T is the master key

Experience is the moat. AI can fake expertise by summarising experts and fake authority with a formal tone. It cannot have first-hand experience. So the highest-value content is "we tested this, here is what happened", not "10 tips for X". Original data, case studies, named authors with real credentials and bios, and honest first-hand insight. This is what Google, the AI models and Reddit users all reward.

Reminder: first-hand experience claims are only used when the client has supplied the data in `approved-facts.md`. Real experience, evidenced. Never invented.

## Technical checklist the SEvO agent enforces

- Let the AI crawlers in via robots.txt: GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended. Blocked means uncitable.
- Schema: Organization, Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product and Review where relevant, all key fields filled.
- HTTPS, fast mobile-first pages, clean hierarchy, sensible internal links, XML sitemap.
- No accidental noindex, no broken links, no key content behind JavaScript.
- llms.txt: nice idea, low adoption, crawlers mostly ignore it, no measured benefit as of mid-2026. Do not rely on it. Do not spend client money on it.

## Measure citations, not just clicks

Rankings and pageviews go blind in a zero-click world. Track two tiers:

- **Platform-native metrics:** site conversion rate and scroll depth, YouTube watch time, TikTok engagement, Amazon conversion, Reddit upvotes and mentions.
- **AI visibility metrics:** Brand Share of Voice (how often the brand is named in AI answers) versus Domain Share of Voice (how often the site is the cited source). High brand SOV but low domain SOV means others control the narrative, so invest in your own citable content. Also track citation count, citation sentiment and zero-click surface presence.

Tools the brief rates: HubSpot AI Share of Voice (trusted most, OpenAI partnership), Profound, Semrush AI Toolkit, Ahrefs Brand Radar, Otterly.ai, AthenaHQ.
