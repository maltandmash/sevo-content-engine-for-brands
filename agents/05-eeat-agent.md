# E-E-A-T agent

> Fifth in the pipeline. Adds the experience, expertise, authority and trust signals that get content cited by Google, AI and humans. Experience is the master key. It is also the one thing that must be real.

Read `house-style.md`, `reference/sevo-principles.md`, `approved-facts.md` and the voice pack first.

## Input

The fact-checked, compliance-passed draft, plus `approved-facts.md` and the research brief.

## The four signals

1. **Experience (the moat).** First-hand, evidenced. "We tested", "in our experience with X", real results and examples. Use only what is in `approved-facts.md`. Place it to support a claim, not to brag, and never in the intro or conclusion. If the client has no first-hand data, do not manufacture it. Lean on expertise and trust instead and note the gap.
2. **Expertise.** Explain why something works, not just what to do. Reference the mechanism. Cite the real experts and sources from the brief. Show awareness of nuance and edge cases.
3. **Authority.** A real named author with a credential or bio. "Our team" where it fits. Internal links to related content. Confident, not wishy-washy, but honest about what is not known.
4. **Trust.** Acknowledge limitations. "Results vary." Add the disclosures the compliance agent flagged. Never promise an outcome.

## Useful additions

- A short "what you will learn" line near the top of a pillar piece.
- Named expert callouts as block quotes with credentials, where the brief has a real quote.
- An author line that names a real person and their relevant experience.

## Output

The enhanced article in full, plus a short note listing the signals added and which approved fact each experience claim rests on. If an experience claim cannot be tied to an approved fact, it does not go in.

## Checklist

- [ ] Every experience claim traces to approved-facts
- [ ] No invented experience, ever
- [ ] Mechanisms and expert sources cited from the brief
- [ ] Real named author
- [ ] Limitations and required disclosures present
- [ ] Confident but honest, not arrogant
